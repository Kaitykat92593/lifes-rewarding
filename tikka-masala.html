Tikka Masala Recipe

Being married to someone from Nepal we are always eating curry, lentils, rice, etc. I have always felt that if we can figure out a way to make it at home over going to and Indian Restaurant, I would rather do that. So, we figured out an amazing way to make Tikka Masala, one of my absolute favorites! This can be time consuming but so worth it I promise! We always have leftovers which is my favorite because the sauce just gets better overtime. Tikka Masala

Marinade:

  • 2 Chicken Breasts Cut in Cubes
  • 1 Cup Yogurt
  • 1/2 Lime or Lemom
  • 1 TBSP Ginger Garlic Paste
  • 1 TBSP Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 TBS Cumin Powder
  • 1 TBSP Paprika
  • 1/2 TBSP Turmeric
  • 1/2 TBSP Oil
Mix all together and marinade chicken for two to three hours. Fry in separate pan with a tsp oil flipping frequently so as not to burn.

Cashew Paste

  • 1/2 C Cashews
  • 2 C Hot Water
Soak cashews in hot water for 15 to 20 mins and make a paste roughly around a half cup

Sauce:

  • 2 TBSP Oil
  • 1 Medium Cinnamon Stick
  • 1 Medium Bay Leaf
  • 2 Cardamom Pods
  • 2 Cloves
  • 1/2 TSP Cumin Seeds
  • 1/2 TSP Fennel Seeds
  • 1 Medium Onion Chopped Finely
  • 2 TBSP Garlic Ginger Paste
  • 15 Ounce Tomato Sauce
  • Salt to Taste
  • 1/2 Cup Cashew Paste
  • 1 TBSP Cumin
  • 2 TBSP Coriander
  • 1 TBSP Turmeric
  • 1 TBSP Paprika
  • 1 TSP or More Black Pepper (based on how spicy you like it)
  • 1 TSP Garam Masala
  • 1 TBSP Butter
  • 1 TSP Brown Sugar
  • 1 TBSP Kasoori Methi or Dried Fenugreek Leaves

Heat oil over medium heat add cinnamon stick, bay leaf, cardamom pods, cloves, cumin, and fennel seeds. Heat until light brown. Add onion until light golden in color roughly for 7-8 minutes. Lower temp to 2 and add garlic ginger paste cook for 2 minutes. Raise heat to medium and add tomato sauce and fry for two minutes. Next add cashew paste and stir continuously until oil starts to separate. Next add cumin, coriander, turmeric, paprika, and black pepper. Fry for 2-3 minutes on medium - low heat. Now add cream along with water (depends on how thin you want your sauce). Bring sauce to boil. Add cooked chicken. Add in garam masala, brown sugar, butter, and salt to taste. Add kasoori methi or dried fenugreek leaves just before serving. Serve with rice or naan and enjoy.
